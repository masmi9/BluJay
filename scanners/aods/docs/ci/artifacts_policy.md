## CI Artifacts Policy

This document defines what artifacts are uploaded by CI, why they are needed, and where they live in the repository.

### Goals
- Preserve evidence for gate decisions and debugging (determinism, completeness, accuracy).
- Keep artifacts unmasked and complete per AODS policy, with retention handled by the CI system.
- Keep the repo clean by ignoring generated artifacts in `.gitignore` (uploaded by CI, not committed).

### Artifacts Uploaded
- Quality gate summaries:
  - `ci_quality_gates_*.json`
  - `ci_quality_report_*.txt`
- Detection accuracy gate summary (if dataset present):
  - `artifacts/ci_gates/detection_accuracy/summary.json`
  - `artifacts/ml_thresholds_derived.json`
  - `artifacts/ml_datasets/metrics/thresholds_summary.json`
- Decompilation outputs (when produced):
  - `artifacts/jadx_decompiled/**` (decompiled sources/res/assets/manifest)
  - `artifacts/decomp_validator/**` (completeness validator reports)
- Reporting conversions (when present):
  - `artifacts/reports/sysreptor_report.json`
- UI Playwright reports (dev/prod):
  - `design/ui/react-app/playwright-report-dev`
  - `design/ui/react-app/playwright-report-prod`
- Frida screenshots (best-effort):
  - `docs/ui/images/*`

### Notes
- Execution Path Gate is strict in CI (`AODS_EXEC_PATH_STRICT=1`), and the gate reads `artifacts/run_manifest.json`. This manifest is generated by `ExecutionPathGuard` and is part of the ephemeral workspace.
- Decomp outputs may be large; the workflow may split large uploads or skip if absent.
- The repository’s `.gitignore` excludes these generated paths to avoid committing artifacts.
